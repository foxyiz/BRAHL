QA on Air

What is QA on Air?

QA on Air is the ecosystem behind BRAHL Web — where Creators, QA Hunters, and platform admins meet around a shared testing lifecycle. Creators describe what they ship; automation and AI run bulk coverage; QA Hunters fill gaps with real journeys, screenshots, and recordings; admins watch satisfaction on both sides.

  • Creator workspaces per project (y/<suite>/)
  • Budget split: BRAHL automation vs QA Hunter pool
  • Change requests when your build moves forward
  • Bug-bounty-friendly invites for external teams
FoXYiZ

What is FoXYiZ?

FoXYiZ is the low-code automation engine that powers BRAHL Run and Loop. It follows f(x, y) = z: the engine (f) runs your yPAD files (y) and writes results to z/ — CSV, dashboards, and error logs.

  • y1Plans — what to test (Run=Y/N, tags)
  • y2Actions — steps (xUI, xAPI, xReuse, …)
  • y3Designs — data, locators, env variables
  • f/fStart.json — suite config for verify & smoke runs

Build yPAD → Run FoXYiZ → Analyze z/ → Heal → Loop until verify is green.

Frequently asked questions

Quick answers for Creators, QA Hunters, and admins new to QA on Air, FoXYiZ, and BRAHL.

What is BRAHL?

BRAHL is the quality lifecycle: Build · Run · Analyze · Heal · Loop — plus a final BRAHL report that summarizes verify health, failures, and next steps. In BRAHL Web, each tab maps to a phase; AI optionally assists on Build, Analyze, and Heal, while Run and Loop execute FoXYiZ directly.

How is FoXYiZ different from BRAHL?

FoXYiZ is the automation engine (f(x,y)=z) — it runs yPAD CSVs and produces z/ results. BRAHL is the full product workflow around that engine: authoring plans, running cycles, reading reports, healing test defects, looping until green, and inviting humans for what automation misses.

What is a QA Hunter?

QA Hunters join a Creator's challenge to cover manual user stories, exploratory paths, UX findings, and evidence (screenshots, screen recordings) that FoXYiZ cannot capture alone. They enrich BRAHL reports and can work alongside automation + AI on the same yPAD workspace.

I'm a Creator — how do I start?

Choose the Creator avatar, pick or create a challenge in the top bar, describe your purpose on Build (AI chat or manual), review yPAD tabs (Plans, Actions, Designs), invite QA Hunters if needed, then Run → Loop → Verify and read your BRAHL report.

My app changed — can BRAHL run again?

Yes. Use “This project has changed — I need assistance again” on Build to file a change request (e.g. new login, API docs). That opens Refine with AI, logs the change in chat, and signals that yPADs and QA Hunter stories should be extended for the new release.

Do I need AI turned on?

No. AI assists Build, Analyze, and Heal when enabled and when OPENAI_API_KEY is set in f/.env. With AI off, Creators describe purpose manually and QA Hunter runs FoXYiZ locally — Run and Loop are always FoXYiZ-only regardless of the toggle.

What are yPADs and white pads?

yPAD = your automation package under y/<suite>/: y1Plans.csv, y2Actions.csv, y3Designs.csv. “White pads” is the informal name for Run=Y plans shown on Build — now organized in tabs so you can explore, filter, and edit CSVs in place.

How do QA Hunters get invited and paid?

Creators click Invite QA Hunter, optionally add team name, email, a note, or a bug-bounty flag. QA Hunters switch to the QA Hunter avatar and join open challenges. Budget on Build splits between BRAHL automation and QA Hunter pool — payout preview shows the split; admins track joins and ecosystem satisfaction on this page.

Where do test results live?

FoXYiZ writes timestamped folders under z/ (e.g. z/20260704_114752_qoa_web/) with *_zResults.csv, HTML dashboards, and BRAHL reports. Analyze and BRAHL tabs in the app read from these runs scoped to your active project.

What does QA on Air admin care about?

Ecosystem health: Creators actively building and reaching verify, QA Hunters joining and contributing evidence, fair budget use, and satisfaction on both sides — modestly happy, very satisfied. Live stats on this page come from /api/about/ecosystem.

Our vision

QA on Air — from KK

I built FoXYiZ, QA on Air, and the BRAHL lifecycle because quality should feel human again — a fighting arena where Creators and QA Hunters meet peer-to-peer, launch absolutely perfect-quality apps, and everyone gets a fair return on time and passion.

Mission 0 — complete

Foundation in place

We tested the products end-to-end, assembled the core team, and grew to about 500 people using the arena today — Creators posting challenges, QA Hunters breaking apps, and automation carrying bulk coverage through FoXYiZ.

Mission 1

5,000 motivated builders

Scale the platform from ~500 to 5,000 users who feel interested, welcomed, and genuinely rewarded — not passive spectators, but people who want to come back because the arena respects their effort.

Mission 2

Return on time & passion

Every hour in the arena should feel worth it: Creators see verify green and fair spend; QA Hunters see earnings and craft; Nalanda contributors see XP for lessons, discussions, and invites. Mutual human experience plus automation and AI — that mix is our edge.

Mission 3

Care for every avatar

Creators fund challenges honestly. QA Hunters hunt with evidence. Nalanda shares knowledge — learn paths, teach lessons, discuss, invite friends (single community page — XP for contribution). Budget flows to humans who helped, covers AI costs, and keeps only a modest slice for QA on Air itself.

Mission 4 — roadmap

Depth for Creators — SEO, brand, human signal

Take Creators deeper than pass/fail: SEO reports, branding checks, accessibility and UX narratives, and rich human feedback (recordings, hunt evidence, version diffs) woven into every BRAHL report — so analysis is not just green verify, but launch-ready product quality.

Mission 5 — Atomic 77

Idea to launch — inside the arena

Atomic 77 is our builder plugin: Creators with an app idea get FAQ, voice-assistant style help, and a path through Build → BRAHL → Go/No-Go. Every avatar’s effort — chat, hunts, shares, tokens — flows to the $ tab and admin analytics so we reward time, passion, and contribution like a true community platform.

  • Champion · Contender · Nalanda — experience tags, not gatekeeping; switch roles in one arena.
  • BRAHL it — the verb: Build, Run, Analyze, Heal, Loop, then read the scorecard.
  • Atomic 77 (A77) — idea-to-launch assistant on every avatar; AI on/off; wallet + XP tracking.
  • Three avatars, one arena — switch Creator · QA Hunter · Nalanda anytime.
  • XP (experience points) — weighted by avatar, time, deliverables, and shares; see the calculator on the $ tab.
  • FoXYiZ local-first — Run and Loop stay free on desktop; AI stays modest unless you choose cloud mode.
  • Transparency — cost meter + XP show where money and points go, so trust stays high.
