# Atomic 77 — Idea to launch inside QA on Air

**Atomic 77** is a plugin within **BRAHL Web** (QA on Air) for **Creators who pay to build apps and ideas** — not just test them.

Many paying customers are **builders**: they have an idea, need human help, automation, and AI to scope it, then **BRAHL it** through to launch.

---

## What Atomic 77 does

| Role | Atomic 77 helps with |
|------|----------------------|
| **Creator** | Turn an idea into scope, budget, yPAD, FAQ, launch checklist |
| **QA Hunter** | Explain how to hunt, submit evidence, read BRAHL reports |
| **Nalanda** | FAQ on community page, explain BRAHL, community XP |
| **Admin (voice)** | Platform FAQ, ecosystem health, where to invest next |

**Pipeline:** Idea → Build → Run → Analyze → Heal → Loop → **BRAHL** → Go/No-Go → Launch

Atomic 77 does **not** replace FoXYiZ Run/Loop (no AI in the engine path). It guides **humans** and **yPAD** work; respects **AI on/off** on the top bar.

---

## In the app

- FAQ chips on the **Nalanda** page work without an OpenAI key; full **A77** tab chat needs **`f/.env`** with `OPENAI_API_KEY`.
- Open the **A77** tab (Creator and QA Hunter avatars; embedded FAQ on Nalanda page).
- Chat respects **AI on/off** — when off, scripted FAQ answers only.
- Usage tracked on **$** tab: XP + estimated token/cost slice for admin analytics.
- Skills context: BRAHL, FoXYiZ, user guide, this doc (via `.md` drawer).

---

## Wallet & community

Every avatar action feeds the **$** meter and **XP** ledger:

- BRAHL phases (automation vs human pool)
- QA Hunter deliverables
- Nalanda community shares and lessons
- **Atomic 77** messages and estimated tokens

Admins use `/admin` stats + GTM invites + Atomic 77 usage to see where humans are happy and what to build next.

## Relation to historical Atomic77 suite

The standalone Base44 app (`atomic77.base44.app`) and `y/atomic77/` FoXYiZ suite were early **digital build services** experiments. This plugin brings that **idea-to-launch** story **inside** QA on Air — one arena, one wallet, one BRAHL lifecycle.
